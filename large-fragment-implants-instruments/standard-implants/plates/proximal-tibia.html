Spoon Plate 4.5

Zoom
  • Spoon Plate 4.5. These plates are primarily indicated for pilon fractures that have extensive anterior crushing and provides good posture support.
  • Spoon Plate 4.5s are designed to fit over the anterior crest of the tibia.
  • It should be bent pre-operative to accurately match the anatomy of the segment of bone to be fixed. An oval hole helps in the temporary fixation of the plate before final adjustment and fixation.
  • Holes of the plate allow 4.5 mm cortex screws.
  • Shaft with V-profile for the fixation on the frontal edge of the tibia. Fixation with 4.5mm Cortex screws.

  • Stainless Steel

T-Plate 4.5

Zoom
  • T-Plate 4.5 is primarily indicated for the medial tibial plateau. It is also used in the distal tibia, medial distal femur, and proximal humerus fractures.
  • It is designed to use in metaphyseal regions of bone.
  • It is bent to accurately match the anatomy of the segment of bone to be fixed.
  • The long oval hole is provided for temporary fixation of the plate before final adjustment.
  • Holes of the plate allow 4.5 mm cortex screws.
  • Shaft with a round profile, for the humeral and tibial head. Fixation with 4.5 & 6.5mm screws.

T-Buttress Plate 4.5

Zoom
  • T-Buttress Plate 4.5. These plates are indicated for the lateral plateau fracture of the tibia.
  • It is designed to be used as a buttress plate in areas of thin metaphyseal cortical bone or to prevent the collapsing during the treatment of cancellous defects in that area.
  • Before the surgery, the plate may be bent to accurately match the anatomy of the segment of bone to be fixed.
  • The long oval hole is provided for temporary fixation of the plate before final adjustment and fixation.
  • Holes of the plate allow 4.5 mm cortex screws.
  • With double bend, as a buttress for tibial head fractures. Fixation with 4.5mm & 6.5mm screws.
